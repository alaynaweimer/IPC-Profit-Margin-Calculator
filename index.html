üéØ Why Profit Margins Matter

Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Many business owners focus on how much money comes in, but what really matters is how much you keep.

A business doing $500K in revenue with 5% margins makes less profit than one doing $200K with 20% margins. Your margins tell the true story of your business health.

üìä Reality check: If your net profit margin is below 10%, you're working hard but keeping very little. Every improvement to your margins goes straight to your pocket.

üíº Pro Tips From the Field

  • Track margins monthly, not just annually. Seasonal swings can hide problems. A bad Q4 can wipe out a great Q1-Q3.
  • High revenue with low margins = a job, not a business. If you're working 60 hours/week for thin margins, your hourly rate might be lower than you think.
  • Raising prices is often easier than cutting costs. A 10% price increase goes straight to profit. Most clients won't leave over 10%.
  • Know your "fully loaded" cost. Many owners forget to include their own salary, benefits, and taxes. Include yourself!
  • Watch margin trends, not just snapshots. Is your margin improving or declining? That trend matters more than any single number.
